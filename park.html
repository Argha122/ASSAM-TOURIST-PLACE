NEHRU PARK

Nehru Park is an urban park situated in the Pan Bazaar area of Guwahati, India.In 1995, the Government of Assam acquired land from Christ Church, Guwahati for the development of a public park, later named Nehru Park after former Prime Minister of India, Jawaharlal Nehru.The park was inaugurated by then-Chief Minister of Assam, Tarun Gogoi on 12 June 2004.The park featured a lush lawn, a playground for children, a jogging track, an amphitheatre, a rock garden, a simulator ride, and a few man-made ponds. It also featured forty-five different concrete sculptures depicting various dance forms of Assam, including Bihu, Ojapali and Jhumair.ATV rides inside the park compound were later introduced
